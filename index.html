Welcome to my webpage!

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Biomathematics at Florida State University, advised by Dr. Washington Mio .

My research lives at the intersection of geometry, topology, and data science. I am interested in developing mathematically principled tools for analyzing complex data that do not naturally fit into classical Euclidean frameworks. Much of my work focuses on metric-measure spaces, dimensionality reduction, and geometrical/topological data analysis, with applications ranging from neural spike train data to biological shape analysis.

What motivates me most is the idea that abstract mathematical structure can reveal meaningful patterns in real-world data—especially in settings where traditional statistical tools struggle. I enjoy working on problems that sit between theory and application, and I care deeply about building methods that are both mathematically rigorous and practically useful.

This webpage is a place where I share my research, talks, and ongoing projects, but also a snapshot of who I am as a mathematician and researcher.
